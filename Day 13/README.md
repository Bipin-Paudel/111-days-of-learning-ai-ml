# Day 13: Binomial Distributions and Probability Density

**🚀 Day 13 of the 111 Days of Learning challenge is complete!**

Today I explored how probability helps estimate real success rates from limited data, and why continuous probability works differently from discrete probability:

- 🎲 **Binomial Distributions:** How to model repeated trials and estimate the likelihood of outcomes.
- 📊 **Success Rate Estimation:** How observed results help infer a true underlying probability.
- 📈 **Probability Density Functions:** Why probability in continuous math comes from area under the curve.
- 🧠 **Why Probability Can Be Zero but Not Impossible:** How a single exact value can have zero probability in a continuous setting.

This made probability feel much more grounded and intuitive. 💡
